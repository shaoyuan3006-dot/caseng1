📋 Case Background:

An 85-year-old male, currently residing in a long-term care facility, with a medical history of stroke, hypertension, and benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH). Following his first stroke one month ago, he presents with dysarthria and has an indwelling nasogastric (NG) tube.

On January 26, he was admitted to the emergency department due to abdominal pain. The physician suspected gastrointestinal bleeding caused by Aspirin and Clopidogrel; consequently, both medications were suspended for three days. On January 29, Aspirin was resumed with gastroprotective Esomeprazole added. An effervescent Acetylcysteine tablet was prescribed for phlegm management.

On February 2, the patient may be discharged if the NG tube is removed. As a pharmacist, you must provide guidance to facility caregivers on the proper use of thickeners to prevent aspiration.
